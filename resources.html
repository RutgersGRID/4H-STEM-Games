Free Resources

A curated list of free tools and assets to help you build your own amazing STEM games.

Game Development & Prototyping

Unity

A powerful, industry-standard game engine for creating 2D and 3D games. The personal plan is free for students and hobbyists.

Twine

An open-source tool for creating interactive, non-linear stories. Perfect for beginners, as no coding is required to start.

Ren'Py

A visual novel engine that lets you create interactive stories with words, images, and sounds. Free for commercial and non-commercial use.

Scratch

A visual programming language from MIT where you can create your own interactive stories, games, and animations using block-based coding.

Minecraft Education

A game-based learning platform that promotes creativity, collaboration, and problem-solving in an immersive digital environment.

Roblox Education

Leverage the popular Roblox platform to create educational experiences, teaching everything from coding and physics to digital citizenship.

Dextrous

A prototyping tool for designing and playtesting physical card games and board games directly in your browser.

AR/VR Development

Adobe Aero

Design and share immersive augmented reality experiences without any coding. Great for bringing 3D models into the real world.

8th Wall

A powerful platform for creating Web-based Augmented Reality (WebAR) that works in a browser, no app required.

3D Modeling & Animation

Blender

A completely free and open-source 3D creation suite. It supports the entirety of the 3D pipeline—modeling, rigging, animation, simulation, rendering, and more.

Polycam

A 3D capture app that lets you create high-quality 3D models from photos using your phone. Great for photogrammetry.

Adobe Mixamo

A free online service that lets you upload a 3D character, automatically rig it, and apply thousands of motion-captured animations.

Art & Design Tools

Krita

A professional, free, and open-source painting program. Excellent for creating 2D game art, textures, and concept art.

GIMP

A free and open-source raster graphics editor used for image manipulation, editing, and creating UI elements or sprites.

Canva

An easy-to-use online graphic design platform to create UI elements, promotional materials, and presentations for your game.

Free Asset Libraries

OpenGameArt

A massive library of free, public domain art, sound, and music assets created by the community for game developers.

Kenney.nl

Offers thousands of high-quality, game-ready assets (2D, 3D, UI, audio) under a very permissive public domain license (CC0).

Itch.io

A marketplace for indie games that also has a huge section for free game assets, including sprites, tilemaps, and more.

Audacity

A free, open-source, cross-platform audio software for multi-track recording and editing. The go-to tool for basic audio work.

Moby Gratis

A resource of free music for independent, non-profit, and student projects, provided by the musician Moby.

Freesound

A collaborative database of Creative Commons licensed sounds. A great place to find specific sound effects for your game.

Project Management & Collaboration

Figma

A collaborative interface design tool for creating user interfaces, interactive prototypes, and design systems. Widely used for UI/UX design in games.

Google Docs

Essential for writing game design documents (GDDs), scripts, and collaborating on text-based plans in real-time.

Miro

An online collaborative whiteboard perfect for brainstorming ideas, creating flowcharts, and visualizing game mechanics with your team.

Notion

A powerful all-in-one workspace for notes, tasks, and project management. Great for keeping your game project organized.

Industry Knowledge

GDC Vault

The official content library of the Game Developers Conference. Many talks from past events are available for free, offering incredible insights from industry professionals.
