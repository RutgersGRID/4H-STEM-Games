Get Involved

Students around the world are using game design and technology to tackle real-world problems. Get inspired by these incredible initiatives and find programs where you can make a difference.

NYC Net Zero Minecraft Challenge

Overview: Middle and high school students redesigned NYC neighborhoods in Minecraft Education to meet net-zero carbon goals.

Impact: Fostered environmental awareness, urban planning literacy, and collaboration between students, educators, and city planners.

Positive Change: Encouraged youth to think critically about sustainable design and climate change while presenting actionable ideas to city officials.

Games for Change Student Challenge

Overview: A national competition where middle and high school students create digital games about social issues like climate change, mental health, and civic engagement.

Impact: Supported by organizations like the National Endowment for the Arts and the Department of Education, it has reached thousands of students across U.S. cities.

Positive Change: Developed student civic awareness, digital literacy, and game design skills to advocate for change.

Learn More →

Technovation Girls (AI & App Development)

Overview: While not game-specific, many projects submitted to Technovation are gamified apps addressing STEM and social issues, like a gamified recycling app to encourage proper waste sorting.

Impact: Encourages girls worldwide to use tech for social good with mentorship and support.

Positive Change: Empowers underrepresented youth in tech and fosters entrepreneurial and design thinking.

Rutgers Creation of Games Society (COGS)

Overview: A student-run club at Rutgers University dedicated to researching, learning, understanding, and teaching all aspects of video game development.

Impact: Provides a community for students to collaborate on projects, participate in game jams, and learn new skills from peers and guest speakers.

Positive Change: Fosters the next generation of game developers by providing hands-on experience and a supportive, creative learning environment.
