Careers in Game Development

Game development is a universe of possibilities, and it's not just about programming! Creating a game is a massive team effort that requires artists, writers, managers, and scientists. Whatever your background or passion, there's a place for you. A team with diverse skills, experiences, and perspectives is an unstoppable force for creating innovative, meaningful, and unforgettable games. Your unique background is your greatest asset.

Creative & Design Roles

Game Designer

The architect of fun. They design the core mechanics, rules, and systems that make a game engaging and enjoyable.

Writer

Crafts the game's narrative, dialogue, character backstories, and world lore to create an immersive and compelling story.

Level Designer

Builds the actual levels and environments where the game takes place, focusing on player flow, challenge, and pacing.

Puzzle Designer

Specializes in creating clever and satisfying puzzles that challenge the player's logic, observation, or creativity.

UI/UX Designer

Focuses on the User Interface (UI) and User Experience (UX), ensuring menus and controls are intuitive, accessible, and easy to use.

Art & Visual Roles

Concept Artist

Creates the initial artwork that establishes the visual style and feel of the game's characters, environments, and assets.

Character Designer

Specializes in creating the look and personality of the game's characters, from heroes to villains.

Environmental Designer

Designs the look and feel of the game's world, creating believable and atmospheric settings for the player to explore.

3D Modeler

Builds the three-dimensional models for characters, props, and environments that populate the game world.

Storyboarder

Creates sequential art to visualize key story moments and cutscenes, much like in filmmaking.

Visual Designer

Works on the overall visual language of the game, including graphic design, typography, and branding.

Technical & Production Roles

Developer/Programmer

Writes the code that brings the game to life, implementing game mechanics, AI, physics, and more.

Data Analyst

Analyzes player data to understand behavior, balance game mechanics, and help the team make informed decisions about game updates and design.

Project Manager

Organizes the development process, managing schedules, budgets, and communication to ensure the game is finished on time.

Tester / QA Analyst

Finds and reports bugs, glitches, and design issues to ensure the game is stable, polished, and fun to play.

Technologist

Researches and implements new technologies, tools, and pipelines to keep the development process modern and efficient.

Expert & Support Roles

Subject Matter Expert (SME)

Provides deep knowledge on a specific topic (like history, physics, or biology) to ensure the game is accurate and authentic.

Researcher

Gathers information and references for the team, from historical accuracy for a period piece to scientific principles for a sci-fi game.

Cultural Expert

Advises the team on cultural representation to ensure that stories and characters are portrayed respectfully and authentically.

Accessibility Specialist

Works to ensure the game can be played by people with diverse abilities, implementing features like colorblind modes and remappable controls.

Marketing

Creates trailers, manages social media, and builds excitement to ensure the world knows about the game when it launches.

How STEM Powers Games

Science

From realistic physics engines to accurate simulations of ecosystems or celestial bodies, science provides the rules that make game worlds believable and dynamic.

Technology

This is the backbone of game development. It includes the game engines, programming languages, rendering pipelines, and AI algorithms that make modern games possible.

Engineering

Game development is a massive engineering challenge. It involves building stable systems, optimizing performance for different hardware, and creating robust online networking for multiplayer games.

Math

Math is everywhere in games: from the 3D vector calculations that render graphics to the probability and statistics that balance game economies and create fair, challenging gameplay.
