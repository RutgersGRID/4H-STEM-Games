Industry Profiles

People from different backgrounds and with different approaches are what make games so great. Explore the profiles below to see how unique perspectives and skills—from science to art to journalism—are powerful assets in the world of game development.

Leigh Alexander

Narrative Designer & Author

A prominent journalist and critic turned narrative designer, Leigh Alexander brings a deep understanding of culture, technology, and storytelling to games. Her work often explores the human condition in digital spaces. She is known for her writing on the award-winning games Reigns: Her Majesty and Reigns: Game of Thrones.

"My work is still concerned with the complexities of existing in digital spaces."

Tracy Fullerton

Game Designer, Professor & Director

As the director of the USC Games program, Tracy Fullerton is a foundational figure in game design education. Her work focuses on creating "serious games" that explore complex topics. Her celebrated game, Walden, a game, is an interactive simulation of Henry David Thoreau's experiment in simple living, showing that games can be a medium for philosophy and reflection.

"Find inspiration in ideas and activities that have meaning to them and then see where that leads rather than rely on standard genres."

Zoë Quinn

Game Developer & Writer

Zoë Quinn is a developer known for creating deeply personal and unconventional games. Their most famous work, Depression Quest, is an interactive fiction game that powerfully communicates the experience of living with depression, demonstrating how games can foster empathy and understanding around difficult mental health topics.

Evan Narcisse

Narrative Designer & Comics Writer

With a background as a journalist and critic focusing on pop culture and race, Evan Narcisse now writes for video games and comics. He has worked on major titles like Marvel's Spider-Man: Miles Morales and Marvel's Avengers, bringing authentic and nuanced perspectives to beloved characters and worlds.

Kid Koala (Eric San)

DJ, Composer & Visual Artist

A world-renowned scratch DJ and musician, Kid Koala brings a completely unique background to game development. He composed the dynamic, break-beat soundtrack for the game Floor Kids, a hand-drawn game about breakdancing. His work shows that a deep passion for music and art can be the driving force behind a game's identity.

Dr. Mandë Holford

Professor of Chemistry & Science Communicator

Dr. Holford is a chemist who studies venom from marine snails to create new medicines. Seeing an opportunity to make science more engaging, she co-founded Killer Snails, LLC, an award-winning company that creates games (like the one in our showcase!) that directly use scientific concepts to create fun, educational experiences. Her work is a perfect example of the STEM-to-games pipeline.

Dr. Michael Cosimini

Pediatrician & Game Designer

As a pediatrician, Dr. Cosimini saw a need to better teach complex medical guidelines. He designed Empiric, a card game that teaches doctors and medical students how to prescribe antibiotics correctly. This shows that a deep understanding of a specific STEM field can be the perfect inspiration for a game that solves a real-world problem.

Jenny Jiao Hsia

Game Developer & Artist

Jenny Jiao Hsia is an independent game developer whose work is known for its playful, tactile, and often humorous style. Her games, like Beglitched and Consume Me, explore themes of technology and daily life through unique and charming aesthetics. She often combines her digital work with physical crafts, like sewing plush dolls, showing that inspiration can come from anywhere.

Mistoria Brown

Educational Technologist & Instructional Game Designer

As a PhD student and educator, Mistoria Brown focuses on promoting equity in STEM and creating culturally relevant learning experiences. She designs instructional games and interactive media that bring history and heritage to life, using technology to amplify student voices and create new pathways for representation in education.

"...the past should serve as a connection point rather than merely being memorized."

Derek Yu

Independent Game Developer

A legendary figure in the indie game scene, Derek Yu is the creator of the critically acclaimed roguelike platformer Spelunky and its sequel. His work is a testament to the power of focused, systems-driven design and proves that a single developer can create a game that influences an entire genre and inspires a generation of creators.

"For me personally as an artist, it's about thinking of [game development] as a continuous exploration."
